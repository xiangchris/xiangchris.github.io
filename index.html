Rotisserie Engine

Rotisserie Engine is a 2D scriptable game engine written in C++ and features Lua scripting support. It was developed during the Winter 2025 semester as part of EECS 498: Game Engine Architecture at the University of Michigan. Rotisserie Engine uses SDL for rendering, Box2D for physics, LuaBridge for scripting, and Dear ImGui to render the editor interface.

Windy Wizard

A 2D action puzzle platformer about a wind-wielding wizard retaking their family's castle from monsters. Worked with a team of 4, over a 6 week cycle. Programmed enemy AI, contributed to level design, coordinated with freelancers, edited the game trailer, and experimented with audio. Created in Unity, programmed in C# with Visual Studio, and used Jira to track tasks. Trailer was filmed with OBS and edited in Adobe Premiere Pro.

Slice and Dice

Turn-based strategy game about dicing vegetables with dice. Developed over a 2 week cycle. Programmed all game logic and animations and drew in-game sprites. Created in Unity, programmed in C# with Visual Studio. Drew sprites in Adobe Photoshop.

Rooted in the Stars

Top-down exploration game about exploring the stars and bringing life to planets. Developed in a team of 4 during the 2023 Global Game Jam, over 48 hours. Programmed logic for the player's roots. Created in Unity, programmed in C# with Visual Studio.
